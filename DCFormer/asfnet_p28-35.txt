5. Conclusion

   This paper presents ASFnet, a novel depth-aware architecture for monoc-
ular RGB-based 3D human pose estimation that explicitly models robust
depth features to mitigate depth ambiguity.            By introducing the Adap-
tive Multi-Sampling (AMS) module, our approach effectively extracts pose-
relevant depth information while reducing dependence on noisy 2D pose esti-
mates. Additionally, the Uncertainty-aware Depth Enhancer (UDE) further
refines depth features by adaptively estimating and suppressing uncertainty-
driven noise. The fusion of these enhanced depth cues with 2D pose in-
formation via a Transformer regressor enables stable 3D pose estimation.
Extensive evaluations on the Human3.6M and MPI-INF-3DHP benchmarks
demonstrate that ASFnet achieves competitive performance compared to
state-of-the-art methods, highlighting the potential of explicit depth mod-
eling for advancing monocular 3D human pose estimation.
   Despite the effectiveness of our approach, we acknowledge its limitations.
Although our method effectively alleviates depth ambiguity in monocular
3D human pose estimation, its current single-frame design inherently limits
its capability to handle severe inter-person occlusions commonly found in
multi-person scenarios.
