   We evaluate on three standard benchmarks under established protocols.
Following previous methods [8, 9], on Human3.6M [12], we use subjects S1,

4.2. Implementation Details

   We adopt HRNet-w32 [16] pre-trained on MS COCO [32] as our 2D de-
tector and Depth-Anything V2 [33] for depth estimation. Both models are
kept frozen during training without fine-tuning on 3D pose data. In line with
previous works [9], we use CPN [5] to obtain the input 2D pose to ensure a
fair comparison in the performance evaluation.
   Training Configuration. Experiments are conducted on an NVIDIA
RTX 4090 GPU using PyTorch. The model is trained for 30 epochs with
a batch size of 64 and gradient accumulation over 4 steps (effective batch
size 256). We used the AdamW optimizer with an initial learning rate
of 6.4 × 10−4 , which is decayed by a factor of 0.99 after each epoch. All
grid sampling uses nn.functional.grid sample with align corners=True and
padding mode=’zeros’. For our core modules, the AMS module employs
N2 = 4 heads with N1 = 5 sample points per head, a fusion dimension
Cfuse = 128, and samples from S = 4 image feature scales. In the UDE
module, the weight for the probabilistic depth loss is set to λd = 1 × 10−5 .
