Abstract

Monocular RGB-based 3D human pose estimation has broad applications
in human-centric scenarios. Mainstream methods follow a 2D-to-3D lifting
pipeline, that first infers a 2D pose from RGB images then lifts it to 3D.
However, this pipeline still struggles with depth ambiguity, despite recent
advances in implicitly inferring depth cues from RGB images or temporal
information from 2D pose sequences. Inspired by recent advances in ded-
icated depth estimation, this paper aims to explicitly incorporate depth
cues through estimated depth maps to reduce depth ambiguity.                       Never-
theless, such estimated depth maps often suffer from substantial irrelevant
background and noise. To address these two issues, we propose the Adap-
tive Sample and Fusion Network (ASFnet). First, we present an Adaptive
Multi-Sampling (AMS) module that utilizes estimated 2D poses as guid-
ance to focus on pose-relevant depth information. To reduce the influence of
2D pose noise, AMS adaptively adjusts sampling points and weights using
both the depth map and 2D pose, focusing on informative regions. Sec-

1. Introduction

   Monocular RGB-based 3D human pose estimation aims to infer 3D poses
from single-view images, playing a vital role in human-centric applications
such as content generation [1, 2] and action recognition [3, 4]. With the ad-
vancement of robust 2D pose detectors [5] and the availability of large-scale
3D motion capture datasets [6], the dominant 2D-to-3D lifting pipeline first
infers 2D poses from RGB images and then lifts them to 3D. However, this
pipeline remains fundamentally ill-posed due to depth ambiguity—multiple
3D poses can project to identical 2D observations. While existing methods
have made progress, those relying solely on 2D keypoints [7, 8] or spatial
context features [9] fail to resolve this ambiguity. Even methods that employ
explicit depth modeling [10, 11] are constrained by coarse ordinal relations
and simply fuse the estimated depth cues with 2D poses, which cannot pro-
vide the precise depth information required for 3D pose estimation.

First, to extract pose-relevant information from the whole depth map, a
straightforward strategy is to sample the depth map at estimated 2D joint
locations. Inaccuracies in the 2D pose can lead to misaligned sampling, prop-
agating erroneous depth features to the 3D pose. Second, the depth maps
themselves inevitably contain noise due to estimation errors, especially un-
der challenging conditions like motion blur or occlusion. To address these
challenges, we propose a novel depth-aware framework whose core novelty
lies in the synergistic combination of two dedicated modules, each designed
to tackle a distinct fundamental weakness. As illustrated in Figure 1 (b), our
solution directly maps a dedicated module to each challenge: the Adaptive
Multi-Sampling (AMS) module tackles spatial misalignment by dynamically

adjusting sampling points around estimated 2D joints to extract reliable
pose-relevant depth features. Concurrently, the Uncertainty-aware Depth
Enhancer (UDE) module suppresses depth noise by explicitly modeling the
uncertainty of each joint’s depth features, self-adaptively emphasizing reli-
able cues and suppressing noisy ones. By integrating these two modules,
our framework establishes a robust pipeline that directly addresses the two
distinct weaknesses in depth-aware pose estimation, transforming raw depth
maps into informative and reliable cues.
   Specifically, to address the challenge of extracting reliable pose-relevant
depth features in the presence of noisy 2D poses, AMS adaptively learns a
set of offsets and weights from both the depth map and the estimated 2D
pose, and uses them to perform flexible multi-sampling from the depth map.
This design allows dynamic adjustment of sampling points during training.
By doing so, AMS enables the model to capture rich pose-relevant depth
information while mitigating the impact of errors in the 2D pose estimation.
   Furthermore, to suppress inherent depth noise and improve robustness
under varying qualities of depth estimation. UDE predicts a distribution
over the sampled depth features for each joint by estimating the expected
value as the depth reference and the variance as the corresponding uncer-
tainty. This uncertainty is then utilized to enhance the depth features by
emphasizing reliable information and suppressing noise. To achieve this, we
formulate the refinement process as a heteroscedastic uncertainty learning
task, where the depth distribution of each joint is constrained through a
probabilistic loss function. The resulting variance matrix explicitly quanti-
fies the measurement uncertainty, allowing the model to self-adaptively boost
informative depth cues. By incorporating this uncertainty-aware refinement,
